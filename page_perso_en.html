Research

I work on the detection and characterization of exoplanets and brown dwarfs in the solar neighborhood. I am the scientific lead for the NIRPS infrared spectrograph (installed at ESO's 3.6m telescope in Chile) and SPIRou (at the Canada-France-Hawaii Telescope). I have developed innovative high-precision velocimetry techniques that enable the detection of Earth-mass planets around low-mass stars. More recently, I developed a method to measure stellar temperature variations with unprecedented precision.
